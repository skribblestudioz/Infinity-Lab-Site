Welcome to Infinity Lab!

Infinity Lab is a student-run organization bringing music and joy to seniors and providing learning opportunities to children in communities that need them.

Organization Information

Organization Name: Infinity Lab

Organization Type: Nonprofit Public Benefit Corporation (California)

Mission and Objectives:

Infinity Lab is committed to building a platform that empowers musically talented and tech-savvy youth to serve the community. Through music performance, gaming activities, technology outreach to seniors, and mentorship for children with limited access to educational resources, these young volunteers are encouraged to take initiative in public service while developing leadership, communication, and organizational skills. We engage youth from resource-rich neighborhoods in San Diego and connect them with underserved communities in the southern region. By facilitating the flow of knowledge and opportunities within the same city, we aim to create a positive social impact, reduce educational inequities, and promote long-term community well-being.

Primary Activities

Nursing Home Services
We recruit talented and community-minded youth volunteers and collaborate with local senior homes to provide musical performances for nursing home residents to enrich their cultural and spiritual lives. Organize interactive games to enhance the physical and mental well-being and social connections of the elderly. Children's Education at Libraries
We recruit talented and community-minded youth volunteers and collaborate with local libraries to offer free classes or extracurricular activities in areas with limited access to educational resources. These programs include, but are not limited to, music, art, and STEAM, aiming to enrich children's after-school experiences, spark their interests and hobbies, broaden their horizons, and inspire early career exploration.

Infinity Lab is a California-based nonprofit public benefit organization. It has not applied for 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status and therefore is not eligible to sponsor the President’s Volunteer Service Award. Volunteer service hours recorded by the organization may or may not be accepted by other institutions, depending on their individual policies. The organization will track volunteer hours after each service and share records with each volunteer.Official members may access their service records at any time. If there is a discrepancy, a correction request must be submitted within a week of the new record’s release. No changes will be made after that period. Service hours for guest performers will be documented and provided upon request, after confirmation by the individual.
